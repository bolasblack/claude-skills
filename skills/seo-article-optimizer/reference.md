## A) Title & Lead Structure (By Intent)

### Informational
- Definition + audience pain point + 3 sub-problems you'll solve
- Example: "What is X? Learn how to solve [pain point] with our step-by-step guide covering [topic 1], [topic 2], and [topic 3]."

### Commercial
- Comparison dimensions + scenario + recommendation logic
- Example: "X vs Y: Which is better for [use case]? We compare features, pricing, and performance to help you decide."

### Transactional
- Clear "who it's for / not for" + key specs/pricing/steps
- Example: "Get started with X in 5 minutes. Perfect for [target audience], this guide walks you through setup, pricing ($X/mo), and first steps."

## B) Internal Linking Strategy

Each article should have at least:
- **1 early link**: Within first 20% of content, pointing to key conversion page
- **1 mid-content link**: Pointing to features/case studies/documentation
- **1 CTA link in conclusion**: Pointing to signup/trial/contact

### Anchor Text Best Practices
- Use descriptive, keyword-rich anchors
- Avoid generic "click here" or "read more"
- Vary anchor text for the same target URL across articles
- Match user intent (informational anchors for blog posts, action-oriented for product pages)

## C) Trust Signals (E-E-A-T)

### Experience
- First-hand experience with the topic
- Personal anecdotes or case studies
- Screenshots, demos, or original research

### Expertise
- Author bio with relevant credentials
- Technical depth appropriate to the topic
- Accurate, up-to-date information

### Authoritativeness
- Citations to authoritative sources
- Links from other reputable sites (earned over time)
- Recognition in the field

### Trustworthiness
- Clear author attribution
- Transparent about affiliations/sponsorships
- Updated dates for time-sensitive content
- Contact information and about page

## D) Readability Metrics for Non-English Content

Flesch Reading Ease doesn't apply perfectly to non-English content. Use these alternative metrics:

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Avg sentence length | < 35 chars (CJK) / < 20 words (EN) | Long sentences reduce comprehension |
| Paragraph length | < 120 chars (CJK) / < 150 words (EN) | Aids scanning |
| Technical term density | Moderate | Add explanations/examples/analogies when high |
| Transition word usage | Adequate | "However", "Therefore", "Additionally" help logical flow |

### Rewrite Example

**Before (too long):**
> In today's rapidly evolving digital transformation landscape, businesses need to continuously update and upgrade their technology infrastructure to ensure they can maintain a leading position in fierce market competition while meeting the ever-increasing expectations and demands of customers.

**After (clearer):**
> Digital transformation requires continuous tech infrastructure updates. This helps businesses stay competitive and meet growing customer expectations.

## E) Featured Snippet Format Guide

### Definition snippet (40-60 words)
```
[Term] is [definition that directly answers the search query].
It [key characteristic 1], [key characteristic 2], and [key characteristic 3].
```

### List snippet
```
## How to [Task]

1. **Step 1**: [Brief description]
2. **Step 2**: [Brief description]
3. **Step 3**: [Brief description]
...
```

### Table snippet
```
| Feature | Option A | Option B |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Price   | $X/mo    | $Y/mo    |
| Users   | 5        | Unlimited|
| Storage | 10 GB    | 100 GB   |
```

## F) Meta Title & Description Templates

### Blog post
- Title: `[Primary Keyword]: [Benefit/Promise] | [Brand]`
- Description: `Learn [topic]. This guide covers [key point 1], [key point 2], and [key point 3]. [CTA].`

### Product page
- Title: `[Product Name] - [Key Benefit] | [Brand]`
- Description: `[Product] helps you [benefit]. Features include [feature 1], [feature 2]. [CTA with urgency].`

### Comparison page
- Title: `[Option A] vs [Option B]: [Decision Factor] ([Year])`
- Description: `Comparing [A] and [B]? See our detailed analysis of [factor 1], [factor 2], and pricing to make the right choice.`

## G) Search Intent Types & Optimization Strategies

| Intent | User Goal | Content Strategy | Keyword Signals |
|--------|-----------|------------------|-----------------|
| Informational | Learn/understand | Tutorials, guides, explainers | "what is", "how to", "why" |
| Commercial | Research/compare | Comparisons, reviews, lists | "best", "vs", "review", "top" |
| Transactional | Buy/act | Product pages, pricing, signup | "buy", "price", "discount", "download" |
| Navigational | Find specific page | Brand pages, homepage | Brand name, product name |

## H) Best Practices Core Principles

1. **User first**: Write for users, not for search engine keyword stuffing
2. **Specific & actionable**: Give copy-paste ready advice, not vague principles
3. **Intent matching**: Understand search intent, content format must match user expectations
4. **Continuous updates**: Mark update dates for time-sensitive content, regularly refresh outdated info
5. **Data-backed**: Cite authoritative sources, provide original data or case studies for credibility

## I) Recommended Word Count by Content Type

| Content Type | Word Count | Notes |
|--------------|------------|-------|
| Simple definition/concept | 500-800 | Quick answer, focus on clarity |
| How-to guide/tutorial | 1000-1500 | Detailed steps, with visuals |
| In-depth analysis/comparison | 1500-2500 | Comprehensive, data-supported |
| Authority guide/pillar | 2500-5000 | Full topic coverage, rich internal links |

> Note: Word count is just a reference. Content quality and completeness matter more than word count.
